IT IS 1888. YOU ARE DRIVING THE LAST UNTAINTED HERD OUT OF SAN ANTONIO TOWARD A FOG-SHROUDED HAVEN IN NEVADA KNOWN AS THE SILVER FOLD. BY DAY THE TRAIL STILL PRETENDS TO BE A NORMAL WESTERN FRONTIER. BY NIGHT THE WORLD SLIPS INTO THE VEIL, WHERE SHADOWS WALK, RIVERS TURN BLACK, AND THE CATTLE HEAR THINGS YOUR CREW PRAYS THEY NEVER WILL.

DEADWOOD TRAIL IS A SUPERNATURAL CATTLE-DRIVE SURVIVAL VARIANT. YOUR PRIMARY SCORE IS THE NUMBER OF CATTLE YOU DELIVER, NOT THE NUMBER OF PEOPLE YOU KEEP COMFORTABLE. FEAR IS THE PRESSURE THE WORLD PUTS ON YOUR CREW. MORALE IS THEIR ABILITY TO HOLD TOGETHER WHEN THE DARK STARTS TALKING BACK.

THE WAGON IS MORE THAN WOOD AND IRON. IT IS SANCTITY ON WHEELS. IF IT ROTS, THE CREW FEELS EXPOSED. IF FEAR GETS TOO HIGH, HOWLING NIGHTS AND STORMS CAN TURN INTO STAMPEDES THAT TEAR THROUGH YOUR HERD.

EL PASO IS THE LAST SAFE LIGHT ON THE TRAIL. REACHING IT SHOULD FEEL LIKE A MERCY. LEAVING IT SHOULD FEEL LIKE A CHOICE.
